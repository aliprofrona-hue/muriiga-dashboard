JMAC Analysis

Mandates increasingly require evidence-based planning. JMAC should provide operational analysis for PoC and stabilisation. In practice, JMAC is overloaded with reporting tasks and lacks integrated access to behavioural and simulation data.

‚ö† Analytic Overload
Increased mandate demands not matched by JMAC staffing or predictive tools. Limited real-time ISR fusion for CONOP rehearsal.
JSOC / CONOPs Implications

Mandate language implies protective patrols, quick reaction, stabilisation support, and DDR facilitation. Without calibrated force posture and ISR, CONOPs become reactive.

Draft Security Council Resolution Language ‚Äî MONUSCO

The following clauses are ready for insertion into a MONUSCO mandate renewal to convert broad tasks into actionable, fundable requirements.

Clause 1 ¬∑ Mandate-to-Competency Requirement
"Requests the Secretary-General, in coordination with the United Nations Department of Peace Operations and the MONUSCO leadership, to translate the mission's mandated tasks into a set of measurable competencies and scenario taxonomies to be used for standardised pre-deployment certification and mission training; and to submit a report on the proposed competency framework within 90 days."
Training GapDoctrine Translation
Clause 2 ¬∑ Analytic Capacity & JMAC Resourcing
"Calls upon Member States to provide targeted support and resources to strengthen the Joint Mission Analysis Centre (JMAC), including access to mission-level behavioural and simulation data, ISR fusion capabilities, and analytic tools necessary for predictive risk assessments; requests the Secretary-General to provide a capability-gap and costed resourcing plan within 60 days."
JMACISR
Clause 3 ¬∑ Capability-Matching & Surge Readiness
"Encourages Member States to provide surge-capable units, tactical airlift, and ISR assets aligned to MONUSCO's operational concept of operations (CONOPs), and requests the Secretary-General to produce a prioritised list of capability shortfalls with associated budget implications within 90 days."
ISRForce Posture
Clause 4 ¬∑ Training Certification & Pilot Authorisation
"Authorises the Secretary-General to pilot standardised, measurable pre-deployment readiness assessments, including scenario-based decision testing and behavioural diagnostics, in partnership with willing troop-contributing countries and regional organisations; requests a pilot report and recommendations for wider adoption within 6 months."
Pre-deploymentCertification
Clause 5 ¬∑ Data Governance & Sovereignty
"Requests that all data collected for training, analytics, and certification be governed by clear data-protection, anonymisation, and institutional ownership arrangements that preserve UN/AU data sovereignty, ensure informed consent, and enable transparent algorithmic explainability."
